Web Development

Learning by building, fixing, and improving real systems

My journey into web development started with a simple frustration: ideas that looked good on paper often failed when real users interacted with them. I wanted to understand why things broke, loaded slowly, or confused users, so I started building complete websites rather than isolated components.

Across academic and personal projects, I handled page structure, layout, responsiveness, and basic backend logic. This forced me to think beyond visuals and understand how design, content, and functionality interact in real environments.

How I Actually Work

I typically begin by sketching page flows and wireframes to clarify how users move through a site. From there, I build layouts using semantic HTML and CSS, focusing on readability, spacing, and responsiveness rather than flashy effects.

JavaScript is used where it adds value, such as form validation, dynamic content loading, or small interactive elements. I test continuously on different screen sizes and fix issues as they appear instead of treating responsiveness as a final step.

For selected projects, I worked with PHP and MySQL to handle form submissions, user authentication, and basic data storage. While I am not a backend specialist, this experience helped me design front ends that connect to real systems rather than static mockups.

What I Learned From Building

One of the biggest lessons was that small technical decisions have a large impact on user experience. Poor structure leads to confusing navigation. Heavy assets slow down engagement. Missing accessibility considerations silently exclude users.

Through trial, feedback, and iteration, I learned to simplify layouts, reduce unnecessary features, and prioritise clarity over complexity. These lessons influenced later projects such as EcoWander, where usability mattered as much as the idea itself.

My Approach

I do not approach web development as writing code for its own sake. I treat it as a problem-solving process where the goal is to make something usable, reliable, and understandable for real people.

I am still learning, and I actively seek feedback to improve how I structure code, organise projects, and design interfaces. What matters most to me is building things that work properly and improving with every iteration.

Netflix Business Model Analysis

University of Southampton | Mar 2025

Project Overview

This comprehensive strategic analysis project examined Netflix's business model using Osterwalder's Business Model Canvas framework, comparing it against major streaming competitors Disney+ and HBO Max. The analysis explored Netflix's $17B content investment strategy, competitive positioning, revenue model sustainability, and strategic differentiation in the highly competitive streaming entertainment market. Through detailed research and comparative analysis, I identified key success factors, competitive advantages, and strategic challenges facing Netflix in the evolving digital entertainment landscape.

What I Did - Detailed Analysis Process

  • Business Model Canvas Analysis: Applied Osterwalder's Business Model Canvas framework to systematically analyze Netflix's nine key business components: customer segments, value propositions, channels, customer relationships, revenue streams, key resources, key activities, key partnerships, and cost structure
  • Competitive Benchmarking: Conducted detailed comparative analysis of Netflix against Disney+ and HBO Max across all business model dimensions, identifying competitive advantages, weaknesses, and strategic positioning differences
  • Content Strategy Analysis: Examined Netflix's $17B annual content investment strategy, analyzing the balance between original content production and licensed content, regional content strategies, and content portfolio diversification
  • Market Research: Gathered and analyzed industry data on subscriber numbers, revenue trends, market share, customer demographics, and competitive dynamics from multiple credible sources including financial reports, industry publications, and market research databases
  • Financial Analysis: Evaluated Netflix's revenue model, pricing strategies, subscriber acquisition costs, customer lifetime value, and profitability metrics to assess business model sustainability

Key Findings & Strategic Insights

Netflix's Competitive Advantages:

  • Data-Driven Content Strategy: Netflix's sophisticated recommendation algorithm and viewer data analytics enable highly targeted content production and personalization, resulting in higher engagement and retention rates
  • Original Content Leadership: $17B annual investment in original content creates exclusive value proposition and reduces dependency on licensed content, with hit shows driving subscriber growth
  • Global Scale & Localization: Presence in 190+ countries with localized content production in multiple regions provides diversified revenue streams and competitive moat
  • Technology Infrastructure: Superior streaming technology, adaptive bitrate streaming, and content delivery network ensure excellent user experience across devices and network conditions
  • Brand Strength: Netflix is synonymous with streaming, enjoying high brand recognition and customer loyalty built over years of market leadership

Competitive Challenges:

  • Intensifying Competition: Disney+ leveraging franchise content (Marvel, Star Wars, Pixar) and HBO Max offering premium HBO content create strong competitive pressure
  • Content Cost Inflation: Rising content production costs and competition for talent increase pressure on profitability
  • Market Saturation: Slowing subscriber growth in mature markets like North America requires focus on international expansion and revenue optimization
  • Password Sharing: Widespread account sharing impacts revenue potential and requires strategic response
  • Ad-Free Model Pressure: Traditional ad-free subscription model faces pressure as competitors introduce ad-supported tiers at lower price points

Strategic Recommendations

  • Introduce ad-supported tier to capture price-sensitive customers and diversify revenue streams
  • Implement password-sharing controls while maintaining customer satisfaction
  • Expand gaming content and interactive entertainment to differentiate from competitors
  • Deepen investment in regional content production to strengthen local market positions
  • Explore strategic partnerships for bundling opportunities and customer acquisition
  • Enhance merchandising and licensing opportunities to monetize popular IP

Marketing Channel Simulation

University of Southampton | Nov 2024

Project Overview

This advanced simulation project involved developing a comprehensive discrete event simulation (DES) model using AnyLogic software to optimize marketing return on investment (ROI) across multiple digital marketing channels including paid advertising, email marketing, and social media campaigns. The simulation modeled customer journeys, channel interactions, conversion funnels, and budget allocation scenarios to identify optimal marketing mix strategies that maximize ROI while considering resource constraints, market dynamics, and channel-specific performance characteristics.

What I Did - Detailed Simulation Development

  • Problem Definition & Scope: Defined simulation objectives to optimize marketing budget allocation across paid ads (Google Ads, Facebook Ads), email marketing, and social media campaigns to maximize overall ROI
  • Conceptual Model Design: Designed conceptual model mapping customer acquisition funnel from awareness through consideration to conversion, modeling how customers interact with different marketing channels
  • Data Collection & Analysis: Gathered industry benchmark data on channel-specific metrics including cost-per-click (CPC), click-through rates (CTR), conversion rates, customer lifetime value (CLV), and channel attribution patterns
  • AnyLogic Model Implementation: Built discrete event simulation model in AnyLogic using agent-based modeling for customers, process flows for channel interactions, and state charts for customer journey stages
  • Channel Performance Modeling: Modeled each marketing channel with unique characteristics including reach potential, cost structures, conversion rates, time delays, and interaction effects with other channels
  • Budget Allocation Algorithm: Implemented dynamic budget allocation algorithm that adjusts spending across channels based on real-time performance metrics, diminishing returns curves, and optimization objectives

Key Findings & Insights

Optimal Budget Allocation Strategy:

  • Paid Advertising (45%): Highest allocation to paid ads due to scalability and immediate results, with emphasis on Google Ads for high-intent keywords
  • Email Marketing (30%): Significant investment in email due to high ROI and customer retention benefits, particularly for nurturing leads and repeat purchases
  • Social Media (25%): Balanced investment in organic content creation and paid social amplification to build brand awareness and community engagement
  • Overall ROI Improvement: Optimal allocation strategy increased overall marketing ROI by 35% compared to equal distribution baseline

Channel Performance Insights:

  • Multi-Channel Synergy: Customers exposed to 3+ channels had 2.5x higher conversion rate than single-channel exposure
  • Email Marketing Efficiency: Email consistently delivered highest ROI per dollar spent but had limited scalability due to list size constraints
  • Paid Ads Scalability: Paid advertising offered greatest scalability but required careful management to maintain positive ROI at higher budgets
  • Social Media Long-Term Value: Social media showed lower immediate ROI but higher long-term customer lifetime value through community building

Public Sector IT Failure Analysis

University of Southampton | Nov 2024

Project Overview

This in-depth case study analysis examined the failure of Health Waikato's Strategic Management System (SMS) implementation, a large-scale public sector IT project that was ultimately terminated after significant time and cost overruns. The analysis applied multiple analytical frameworks including the balance sheet framework, agile methodology assessment, and the ABC (Antecedent-Behavior-Consequence) organizational change model to identify root causes, systemic failures, and lessons learned.

What I Did - Detailed Analysis Process

  • Case Background Research: Conducted extensive research on the Health Waikato SMS project including project scope, objectives, timeline, budget, stakeholders, technology choices, and the sequence of events leading to project failure
  • Balance Sheet Framework Application: Applied the balance sheet framework to systematically assess project assets (capabilities, resources, stakeholder support), liabilities (risks, constraints, opposition), and stakeholder equity (interests, power, influence)
  • Agile Methodology Evaluation: Analyzed the implementation of agile methodology in the public sector healthcare context, evaluating whether agile practices were appropriately adapted and whether organizational culture supported agile principles
  • ABC Model Analysis: Applied the Antecedent-Behavior-Consequence model to understand organizational change dynamics, identifying triggers for change, behaviors of stakeholders, and consequences that contributed to project trajectory
  • Root Cause Analysis: Conducted systematic root cause analysis using techniques like "5 Whys" and fishbone diagrams to identify fundamental issues beyond surface-level symptoms

Key Findings - Root Causes of Failure

Technical & Project Management Issues:

  • Scope Creep: Continuous expansion of project scope without corresponding adjustments to timeline and budget, driven by stakeholder demands and changing requirements
  • Underestimated Complexity: Significant underestimation of technical complexity, integration challenges, and data migration requirements for large-scale healthcare system
  • Vendor Management: Inadequate vendor oversight, unclear accountability, and misaligned incentives between vendor and client
  • Testing Deficiencies: Insufficient testing, particularly integration testing and user acceptance testing, leading to quality issues

Organizational & Change Management Issues:

  • Stakeholder Resistance: Significant resistance from clinicians and frontline staff who were inadequately engaged in design and felt system didn't meet their needs
  • Change Management Failure: Weak change management program failing to prepare organization for transformation, inadequate training, and poor communication
  • Clinical Engagement: Insufficient involvement of clinical staff in requirements definition and design, resulting in system that didn't support clinical workflows
  • Leadership Turnover: High turnover in project leadership and executive sponsors creating inconsistent direction and loss of institutional knowledge

Strategic Recommendations

For Future Public Sector IT Projects:

  • Realistic Planning: Ensure realistic project scoping, timeline, and budget estimation based on comparable projects and independent expert review
  • Robust Governance: Establish clear governance structures with defined decision rights, escalation paths, and independent oversight mechanisms
  • Stakeholder Engagement: Invest heavily in stakeholder engagement especially frontline users, involving them in requirements, design, and testing
  • Change Management: Implement comprehensive change management program addressing culture, training, communication, and organizational readiness
  • Incremental Delivery: Favor incremental delivery of value over big-bang implementations, allowing for learning and course correction
  • Independent Assurance: Engage independent experts for project health checks, providing objective assessment to governance bodies
