  • AnyLogic Model Implementation: Built discrete event simulation model in AnyLogic using agent-based modeling for customers, process flows for channel interactions, and state charts for customer journey stages, incorporating stochastic elements to represent real-world variability

ABC (Antecedent-Behavior-Consequence) Model:

  • Antecedents Identification: Identified triggers and conditions that prompted specific behaviors including organizational pressures, policy mandates, competitive factors, and technology trends
  • Behaviors Analysis: Analyzed actual behaviors of key actors including decision-making patterns, communication practices, conflict resolution approaches, and change resistance
  • Consequences Evaluation: Assessed consequences of behaviors both intended and unintended, including project delays, cost overruns, stakeholder disengagement, and ultimate project termination
  • Feedback Loops: Identified reinforcing and balancing feedback loops that either amplified problems or attempted to correct course

Research & Documentation Methods:

  • Document Analysis: Analyzed official project reports, audit findings, government inquiries, and post-implementation reviews
  • Literature Review: Reviewed academic literature on public sector IT failures, agile in healthcare, and organizational change management
  • Comparative Case Studies: Studied similar cases including NHS National Programme for IT, Queensland Health payroll system, and other notable public sector IT failures
  • Framework Application: Systematically applied analytical frameworks to structure analysis and ensure comprehensive coverage

Key Findings - Root Causes of Failure

Technical & Project Management Issues:

  • Scope Creep: Continuous expansion of project scope without corresponding adjustments to timeline and budget, driven by stakeholder demands and changing requirements
  • Underestimated Complexity: Significant underestimation of technical complexity, integration challenges, and data migration requirements for large-scale healthcare system
  • Vendor Management: Inadequate vendor oversight, unclear accountability, and misaligned incentives between vendor and client
  • Testing Deficiencies: Insufficient testing, particularly integration testing and user acceptance testing, leading to quality issues
  • Agile Implementation Gaps: Superficial agile adoption without genuine cultural transformation, resulting in "agile theater" rather than true agile delivery

Organizational & Change Management Issues:

  • Stakeholder Resistance: Significant resistance from clinicians and frontline staff who were inadequately engaged in design and felt system didn't meet their needs
  • Change Management Failure: Weak change management program failing to prepare organization for transformation, inadequate training, and poor communication
  • Clinical Engagement: Insufficient involvement of clinical staff in requirements definition and design, resulting in system that didn't support clinical workflows
  • Leadership Turnover: High turnover in project leadership and executive sponsors creating inconsistent direction and loss of institutional knowledge

Governance & Political Issues:

  • Weak Governance: Inadequate project governance structures with unclear decision rights, insufficient oversight, and weak escalation mechanisms
  • Political Pressure: Political pressure to demonstrate progress and meet unrealistic deadlines led to premature go-live decisions and inadequate readiness
  • Risk Management: Systematic underestimation and underreporting of risks, with insufficient risk mitigation strategies
  • Accountability Gaps: Unclear accountability and responsibility allocation across multiple stakeholders including health board, IT department, and vendors

Strategic Recommendations

For Future Public Sector IT Projects:

  • Realistic Planning: Ensure realistic project scoping, timeline, and budget estimation based on comparable projects and independent expert review
  • Robust Governance: Establish clear governance structures with defined decision rights, escalation paths, and independent oversight mechanisms
  • Stakeholder Engagement: Invest heavily in stakeholder engagement especially frontline users, involving them in requirements, design, and testing
  • Change Management: Implement comprehensive change management program addressing culture, training, communication, and organizational readiness
  • Agile Adaptation: Adapt agile methodology appropriately for public sector context while maintaining core principles, with focus on cultural transformation not just process adoption
  • Risk Management: Establish rigorous risk management processes with honest risk assessment, transparent reporting, and proactive mitigation
  • Vendor Management: Implement strong vendor management with clear contracts, performance metrics, accountability mechanisms, and alignment of incentives
  • Incremental Delivery: Favor incremental delivery of value over big-bang implementations, allowing for learning and course correction
  • Independent Assurance: Engage independent experts for project health checks, providing objective assessment to governance bodies
