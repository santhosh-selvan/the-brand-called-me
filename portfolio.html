Netflix Business Model Analysis

University of Southampton | Mar 2025

Project Overview

Conducted a 360° analysis of Netflix's business model using Osterwalder's Canvas framework to evaluate its competitive positioning in the streaming wars, with particular focus on content strategy, monetization challenges, and technological advantages.

Detailed Analysis

  • Business Model Deconstruction: Mapped all 9 elements of Osterwalder's Canvas including Key Partners (Sony, AWS, TikTok creators), Revenue Streams (Subscription tiers $7–$20/month, emerging ad business), Cost Structure (70% content spend, $17B/year with 60% flop rate)
  • Competitive Benchmarking: Compared against Disney+ (franchise model) and HBO Max (premium content): Netflix's $25 ad CPM vs. HBO's $40; 3 major franchises vs. Disney's 12
  • Critical Challenges: $16B content debt, 23% creator attrition, rising production costs ($30M/episode for Stranger Things S5)

Key Findings & Conclusion

Strategic Advantages: Unmatched recommendation algorithm (2,000+ taste clusters), global content leadership (25% Korean productions), first-mover binge strategy.
Vulnerabilities: High-risk content model, lagging ad monetization, intense competition.
Recommendations: Optimize ad-tier pricing, expand into interactive media/gaming, develop franchise IP to rival Disney.

Business Model Analysis Competitive Assessment Strategic Recommendation Data Interpretation Financial Valuation

Discrete-Event Simulation (DES)

University of Southampton | Nov 2024 - Jan 2025

Objective

Solve the multi-armed bandit problem in digital marketing with non-stationary conversion probabilities and budget constraints.

Key Achievements

  • Modeled channel decay effects — conversion rates decreased 0.5–1.2% per hour due to audience fatigue
  • Implemented Thompson sampling logic for adaptive budget reallocation
  • Quantified value-at-risk (5% VaR = £3.8/hour) using empirical distributions

Strategic Outcomes

  • Optimal Exploration-Exploitation Balance: 55% Social | 30% Ads | 15% Email
