Gap Denim Danced. American Eagle Tripped. 👖✨

Sep 25, 2025 | Marketing, Case Studies

Not all virality is equal. Some ads make history. Others just make noise.

👉 On August 19, GAP released its new denim campaign with KATSEYE, the global girl group. At first glance, it’s simple: Y2K-inspired denim fits, sharp choreography, and Kelis’ Milkshake. But the brilliance is deeper: each member of KATSEYE symbolizes a different personality, background, and style. Together, they form an iridescent whole. That’s the metaphor. That’s why it worked.

Denim isn’t one-size-fits-all and neither is culture. GAP didn’t just show jeans. It showed range.

👉Now compare that to American Eagle x Sydney Sweeney. Sydney is talented, recognizable, and influential. But AE branded her in the flattest way possible: a pun on “Great Jeans/Genes.” Instead of celebrating individuality, they reduced her to biology. And in 2025, when conversations around beauty standards and representation are louder than ever, the ad backfired. Yes, both campaigns went viral.

But while GAP sparked joy, nostalgia, and inclusivity, AE sparked backlash, think pieces, and political takes. The difference? GAP built a campaign people wanted to share. AE built a campaign people wanted to debate. And in marketing, those two outcomes couldn’t be further apart.

💡 Lesson for us marketers: A spokesperson is never just a person. They’re a symbol. KATSEYE became a metaphor for denim’s diversity. Sydney was reduced to a tagline.

✨ Attention is easy. Affection is earned.

How Netflix Mastered Brand Sentiment Polarity

Sep 18, 2025 | Case Studies, Brand Strategy

The Art of Calculated Risks

Netflix has long been a master of capturing the cultural zeitgeist, but its success isn't just about producing hit shows. It's about strategically managing brand sentiment polarity – the ability to engage with controversial topics and generate passionate debate without alienating its core audience. This case study delves into the mechanics of Netflix's approach, examining how it leverages data, creative freedom, and a deep understanding of its audience to navigate the complex landscape of modern media.

Key Elements of Netflix's Strategy:

  • The Algorithm as a Shield: How Netflix's recommendation engine allows it to cater to diverse and sometimes conflicting tastes simultaneously.
  • Empowering Creators: The role of creative freedom in fostering groundbreaking content that sparks conversation.
  • The Global-Local Playbook: How Netflix balances global brand consistency with localized content strategies to resonate with diverse cultural contexts.

By understanding how Netflix not only survives but thrives in a world of polarized opinions, marketers can learn valuable lessons about brand resilience, audience engagement, and the power of a well-managed content strategy.
